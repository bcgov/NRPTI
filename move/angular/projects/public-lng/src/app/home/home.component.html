OVERVIEW

British Columbia has been the location of natural gas exploration and production for more than 50 years.

Liquefied natural gas, or LNG, is a technical process that involves cooling raw natural gas into a liquid form. As a liquid, it can be transported overseas by ship.

LNG Canada will be the first large-scale export facility of its kind in British Columbia. Once operational, LNG Canada will ship natural gas to Asia and other markets where it will be processed for energy needs.

The Government of British Columbia has statutory responsibilities to ensure the natural gas industry, including the companies involved with LNG Canada and its connecting pipeline - Coastal GasLink - are permitted and compliant with all government legislation and requirements.

This site provides context and access to the multiple layers of authorizations, compliance updates and other items that represent the provincial government’s role as it relates to LNG regulation and oversight. Documents from the BC Energy Regulator and British Columbia’s Environmental Assessment Office can be searched and downloaded, and other natural resource ministries will have a growing presence on the site.
